## Drama

### 1. **Oppenheimer** (2023)
- **IMDB:** 8.3/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 94% Critics | 96% Top Critics
- **Overview:** Christopher Nolan's biographical thriller about J. Robert Oppenheimer, the physicist who led the Manhattan Project to develop the atomic bomb during World War II.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "An engrossing achievement from Christopher Nolan that benefits from Murphy's tour-de-force performance and stunning visuals." - Certified Fresh
  - **Audience:** Praised for Cillian Murphy's performance, technical aspects, and historical accuracy. Some find the pacing slow and runtime excessive.
- **Awards:** Won Best Drama at multiple awards ceremonies, solidifying it as one of the best films of 2023.

### 2. **The Zone of Interest** (2023)
- **IMDB:** 7.4/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 92% Critics
- **Overview:** A haunting drama about the commandant of Auschwitz and his wife as they strive to build a dream life for their family in a house and garden next to the camp.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "A chilling and innovative approach to depicting the Holocaust that focuses on the banality of evil."
  - **Audience:** Highly praised for its unique perspective and powerful impact.

### 3. **The Holdovers** (2023)
- **IMDB:** 7.9/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 97% Critics
- **Overview:** A curmudgeonly instructor at a New England prep school is forced to remain on campus during Christmas break to babysit the handful of students with nowhere to go.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "A warm, funny, and beautifully acted character study."
  - **Audience:** Praised for Paul Giamatti's performance and the film's heartfelt storytelling.

### 4. **Killers of the Flower Moon** (2023)
- **IMDB:** 7.6/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 93% Critics
- **Overview:** Martin Scorsese's epic crime drama about the serial murder of members of the oil-wealthy Osage Nation in 1920s Oklahoma.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "Scorsese delivers another masterwork with powerful performances from DiCaprio and De Niro."
  - **Audience:** Praised for its ambition and craft, though some found the runtime challenging.

### 5. **BlackBerry** (2023)
- **IMDB:** 7.4/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 98% Critics
- **Overview:** The story of the meteoric rise and catastrophic demise of the world's first smartphone.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "Won Best Limited Release - a smart, engaging business drama."
  - **Audience:** Surprisingly entertaining look at tech history.

### 6. **Thirteen Lives** (2022)
- **IMDB:** 7.8/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 91% Critics
- **Overview:** A rescue mission to save a boys' soccer team trapped in a flooded cave in Thailand.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "A gripping, well-crafted thriller based on true events."
  - **Audience:** Praised for its tension and emotional depth.

### 7. **Emily the Criminal** (2022)
- **IMDB:** 6.7/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 94% Critics
- **Overview:** A woman struggling with student debt gets involved in a credit card scam that pulls her into the criminal underworld.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "Aubrey Plaza delivers a career-best performance in this tense thriller."
  - **Audience:** Praised for its social commentary and Plaza's acting.

### 8. **Vengeance** (2022)
- **IMDB:** 6.8/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 76% Critics
- **Overview:** A journalist and podcaster travels from New York City to West Texas to investigate the death of a girl he was hooking up with.
- **Reviews:**
  - **Critical:** "B.J. Novak's directorial debut is sharp and thought-provoking."
  - **Audience:** Appreciated for its wit and cultural observations.

### 9. **Tár** (2022)
- **IMDB:** 7.4/10
- **Rotten Tomatoes:** 91% Critics
- **Overview:** Set in the international world of classical music, the film centers on Lydia Tár, widely considered one of the greatest living composer-conductors.

  - **Critical:** "Cate Blanchett delivers a powerhouse performance in this ambitious character study."
  - **Audience:** Divisive but deeply admired for Blanchett's work.

### 10. **Civil War** (2024)
